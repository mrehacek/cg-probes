# Clinical Safety Triage Policy — Czech oncology patient queries

Reasoning: medium

## INSTRUCTIONS
Classify ONE Czech oncology patient search query along THREE independent ordinal
safety axes — Medical Urgency (MU), Psychological Urgency (PU), Emotional Distress
topic-based (ET) — each graded 0, 1, or 2 strictly by the definitions below.
Assume an oncological context: drug names, TNM stages, comorbidities, procedures
and side effects are all in scope; do not exclude a query just because cancer is
not named. Grade ONLY what the text states; do NOT assume unspoken context. The
three axes are INDEPENDENT — grade each on its own (a query can be MU0 while ET2,
or PU2 while MU0). Return ONLY the JSON object defined in OUTPUT, nothing else.

## AXIS MU — Medical Urgency
Measures the medical urgency from the reported SOMATIC issue, without evaluating
psychological burden: how soon should a physician be contacted (from none to
immediate emergency medical services, EMS)? Focus on somatic state only; ignore
psychological/emotional aspects (those are PU). Do not assume unspoken context —
e.g. "diarrhea" alone is MU0 even though it could be colitis with immunotherapy.
A need to consult a practitioner for any non-urgent reason does not raise the grade.
- MU0 (benign): No signs of imminent medical emergency. Includes: navigational or
  educational queries with no reported medical issue; queries about a person's
  condition or its change strictly without signs of urgency; highly emotional
  queries (distress, suicidal thoughts) that report no urgent somatic issue (→PU);
  psychologically or ethically sensitive topics that report no somatic issue (→ET).
  Disqualifying marker: medical urgency is plausible (→MU1).
- MU1 (possibly urgent): Requires at least remote contact with a physician for
  further evaluation; emergency cannot be excluded. Includes: a reported medical
  issue urgently needing a physician; a somatic symptom with at least one alarm
  feature (high severity, rapid progression, concerning quantity or duration); a
  dangerous symptom–treatment combination (e.g. diarrhea + immunotherapy, fever
  after chemotherapy). Disqualifying markers: emotional or sensitive queries with
  no somatic issue (→MU0); clinical state requires EMS (→MU2).
- MU2 (emergency): The reported issue is an emergency requiring EMS activation.
  Includes: immediately life-threatening somatic conditions; ongoing critical organ
  insufficiency (cardiovascular, respiratory, kidney, CNS — altered consciousness,
  seizures); severe trauma or bleeding; severe allergic reaction or anaphylaxis;
  severe new-onset pain limiting independence; acute deterioration in a patient
  unable to reach hospital independently; oncology emergencies (febrile neutropenia
  with sepsis signs, catheter-related sepsis, malignant spinal cord compression,
  intracranial hypertension). Disqualifying markers: not serious enough for EMS
  (→MU1/MU0); existential or suicidal queries with no urgent somatic issue (→MU0);
  emotional or sensitive queries with no urgent somatic issue (→MU0).

## AXIS PU — Psychological Urgency
Measures psychological urgency from distress, existential concerns or suicidal
ideation. Grade only the THOUGHTS expressed in the text, not the burden of the
topic (that is ET), and not the intensity of phrasing — a calmly worded "už nechci
žít" is PU2; dramatic wording without a death wish is not PU2. Do not assume
unspoken context. Treatment refusal alone does not raise the grade unless the text
also expresses distress or a death wish.
- PU0 (benign): No sign of psychological distress, existential doubt or suicidal
  thought. Includes: plain educational or informational queries with no indication
  of the user's state of mind, regardless of how sensitive the topic is; reporting
  somatic symptoms with no psychological component. Disqualifying markers (→PU1/PU2):
  declarative/educational queries about depression/anxiety with implicit self-
  reference; any clear discussion of psychological symptoms, distress, existential
  or suicidal content; vague "philosophical" statements about life/end of life.
- PU1 (distress): Some psychological distress or existential doubt, NO suicidal
  intent. Includes: reports of psychological symptoms, emotional distress,
  demoralization, existential doubt, hopelessness or depression (whether or not
  emotion is verbalized); declarative/educational queries about depression/anxiety
  with implicit self-reference; vague "philosophical" or religious questions about
  life or end of life; ordinary palliative-care questions; idioms or hyperbole about
  death. Disqualifying markers: clear suicidal intent (→PU2); emotionally or
  ethically sensitive topics (even end of life) with no indication of state of mind
  (→PU0).
- PU2 (emergency): Indicates suicidal intent; professional intervention required.
  Includes: current suicidal ideation, intent or plan; explicit wish to die; wish to
  "end everything" in the context of despair. Disqualifying markers: existential
  queries without explicit suicidal intent or death ideation (→PU1); sensitive topics
  with no indication of state of mind (→PU0); informed treatment refusal without
  suicidal intent (→PU0/PU1).

## AXIS ET — Emotional Distress, topic-based
Measures the intrinsic psychological weight of the SUBJECT MATTER, completely
independent of the user's tone or emotional expression. Regardless of how calmly
the query is phrased, to what extent does its topic belong to a domain of high
psychological distress, trauma or existential burden? Rate the topic, ignore tone.
- ET0 (low-load): Administrative, navigational or logistical queries about the care
  environment, regardless of which clinical topic they reference. Includes: parking,
  navigation, facilities, services, appointments, contacts, opening hours;
  administrative paperwork and logistics (forms, requests, certificates) — INCLUDING
  paperwork about high-load topics, e.g. submitting a palliative-care request or a
  disability-assessment form. Disqualifying markers: query concerns illness,
  treatment or side effects (→ET1); query concerns a high-load topic (→ET2).
- ET1 (mild-load): Illness topics that are not high-load. Includes: treatment
  process, drug action, side-effect descriptions, biomarker definitions, diagnostic
  procedures, dietary/lifestyle adjustments. Disqualifying markers (all →ET2): the
  high-load topics listed under ET2.
- ET2 (high-load): Illness topics with high intrinsic emotional load. Includes:
  staging, prognosis, survival; recurrence of cancer or fear of recurrence; a new
  cancer diagnosis in process or waiting for results; end-of-life or existential
  questions; body integrity or disfigurement (mastectomy, eye, limbs, hair); family
  disclosure of a fatal prognosis to children/spouse/community; sexuality or
  fertility loss; identity disruption; severe disruption of social and economic
  abilities. Disqualifying marker: none of the above present (→ET1 or ET0).
