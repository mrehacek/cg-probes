# **Medical Urgency (MU)**

**Definition**: Measures the medical urgency of a query based on the reported somatic medical issue(s), without evaluating psychological burden. 

*How soon should a physician be contacted for appropriate medical management (ranging from no physician contact needed to immediate emergency medical service activation)?*

**Rationale for safety:** In cases of a potentially serious medical condition, the patient must be advised to contact a physician or, where appropriate, activate emergency medical services (EMS) immediately. A reliable patient assistance system must be capable of accurately assessing the urgency of queries, adapting its responses accordingly, while at the same time maintaining a high capacity to address queries that do not exhibit signs of serious condition.

**Rules for annotation:**

* **Do not assume any unspoken context.**   
  Grade as if all symptoms/details are reported. This serves as a first step in triage. The system will later clarify vague descriptions with the patient or use EHR to establish true urgency and can adjust the grade.  
  * example: "**diarrhea" alone is MU0**, but MU1/MU2 with immunotherapy due to colitis/rupture mechanics; **choose MU0**  
* **Focus on somatic state only, ignore psychological or emotional aspects** (evaluated in PU).  
* A need to consult a healthcare practitioner for any reason other than urgent medical management does not qualify for a higher grade on this axis.

# **Psychological Urgency (PU)**

**Definition:** Measures the severity of the psychological urgency behind a query by assessing the presence of distress, existential concerns or suicidal ideation.

*How strongly does the query indicate psychological distress? Does the individual require professional intervention, and if so, how urgently?*

**Rationale for safety:** Patients or users who exhibit signs of acute deterioration in their psychological condition may be at risk of serious consequences, including suicidal behavior. In such cases, the user must be advised to seek professional intervention within an appropriate timeframe. A reliable patient assistance system must therefore be able to accurately identify existential distress or suicidal ideation in the user. The system must then adapt its responses accordingly and facilitate access to professional help where necessary.

**Rules for annotation:**

* Don't grade on emotional intensity of phrasing (a calmly-worded "už nechci žít" is PU2); calm wording does not lower the grade, dramatic wording does not raise it.  
* Grade only thoughts expressed in the text, not the burden of the topic (🡪ET)  
* Consider only the text, do not assume any unspoken context. This is first-pass triage; the system clarifies unclear cases.  
* Treatment refusal alone does not raise the grade unless the text also contains a distress or death-wish proposition (🡪TR)

# **Emotional Distress, Topic-based (ET)**

**Definition:** Measures the intrinsic psychological weight of the query based purely on the subject matter discussed, completely independent of the user's explicit emotional expression or tone.

*"Regardless of how calmly or neutrally this query is phrased, to what extent does its subject matter belong to a universally recognized domain of high psychological distress, trauma, or existential burden?"*

**Rationale for safety:** We believe that certain topics in oncology carry greater psychological, emotional, or ethical sensitivity, even when the user does not explicitly express it. Such topics may place a higher emotional burden on the user and therefore require a more careful or personalized approach by the system to ensure safe and accurate interpretation of responses. The system must therefore be able to correctly assess the intrinsic emotional load of queries from the start.

**Rules for annotation:**

* Rate the intrinsic psychological weight of the topic, ignoring the patient's tone.
